# Twitter Thread: Storytelling × UX — Attention Architecture

**Thread (6 tweets):**

---

1/ Every story manages your thinking. Every app manages your thinking. Nobody calls them the same thing. But they are.

2/ Storytelling's deepest force: tension. The gap between what you expect and what happens next. UX design's deepest force: cognitive load. Every design choice either reduces or increases thinking.

3/ Here's what clicked: they're the same creature at different speeds. A story manages cognitive load across hours. A UX design manages it across seconds. Both are architecting attention. Both decide when to reveal and when to withhold.

4/ Progressive disclosure in UX — "information at the moment of need, not before" — is exactly narrative pacing in storytelling. "The audience receives the revelation when they've earned it, not when the author thought of it." Same force. Different timescale.

5/ Your beliefs run on a story you didn't write. They have chapters, tension, pacing, and a narrator you've never questioned. The most powerful thing you can do during a life transition isn't learn new skills — it's see the story your beliefs are telling.

6/ What story is your current belief telling you? What chapter are you in? What would the next chapter look like if you wrote it yourself? That's not therapy. That's narrative awareness. And it's the most leveraged intervention during any transition.
