# Thread 04 — The Story That Broke

## Thread

**Tweet 1:**
A Form 4 leaver gets their KCSE results. The score is bad. Everyone asks "so what now?"

That's the wrong question. Here's what's actually happening.

**Tweet 2:**
They're not facing a career problem. They're facing a *narrative collapse*.

"Study hard → good grades → good job" was the story they lived inside. It just broke. They don't need advice. They need a new story — and nobody's handed them the pen.

**Tweet 3:**
Expert problem solvers know: the stated problem is never the real problem. The real problem is always structural. The question isn't "what do I do with a C+?" The question is "who writes the next chapter?"

**Tweet 4:**
Here's what nobody tells students: transitions aren't gaps in your plan. They're moments when the old story can't contain reality anymore. That's not failure. That's the plot turning.

**Tweet 5:**
We're building KIRA to recognize which story just collapsed — and hand the student the pen.

Not "here's what to do." But "here's where your story broke. Now let's write the next one."
