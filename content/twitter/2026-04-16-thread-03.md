# Twitter Thread: The Ceremony Trap

**Thread (8 tweets):**

---

1/ Here's something that should bother you. Agile was designed to be the antidote to the innovator's dilemma — short cycles, inspect and adapt, ship and learn.

But most "agile transformations" ARE the innovator's dilemma playing out in real time.

2/ The innovator's dilemma: the thing that makes you successful makes you blind to the next wave. You optimize so thoroughly for current reality that when the new reality arrives, your architecture can't see it.

3/ Most agile adoptions: the organization feels the need to change. They adopt the visible symbols — standups, boards, retros, sprint planning. But the invisible infrastructure stays untouched. Incentives. Fear. Hidden constraints. The org chart fighting the team topology.

4/ Then they're MORE locked in than before. Because they believe they've already changed. "We're agile now." No, you installed the ceremony without changing the forces.

5/ This exact pattern runs your personal life too. You hit a transition. You adopt the visible symbols of adaptation — choose a course, update your LinkedIn, make a plan. But your belief firmware stays untouched.

6/ And now you're more locked in. Because you believe you've already made your choice. "I'm on track." You're not on track. You installed the ceremony without addressing the invisible forces.

7/ The most dangerous trap isn't not changing. It's believing you've changed when you've only changed the surface. Real transformation is invisible until it isn't.

8/ Your beliefs have an architecture. When did you last inspect it?
