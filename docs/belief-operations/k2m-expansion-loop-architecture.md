# K2M Expansion Loop Architecture
## How Deeper Adoption Creates New Market Fuel

*Status: reconciled against PRD v0.2 - 2026-04-10*

---

## Purpose

This document defines the expansion loop inside the K2M belief operations system.

Expansion is not the same as referral.

Referral asks:

- who will tell others?

Expansion asks:

- how does deeper use create stronger proof, wider trust, and new routes into the market?

Expansion only matters after the student beachhead loop is real.
Per the PRD, K2M does not activate new audiences just because the theory is elegant.

---

## Governing Principle

The strongest penetration asset is often created after retention succeeds.

For K2M, deeper adoption should create:

- stronger identity shift
- stronger proof objects
- stronger alumni signals
- stronger bridge access into new clusters
- stronger content inputs for the next penetration cycle
- clearer evidence for whether teacher, principal, or B2B activation should happen at all

---

## The Expansion Loop

Use this loop as the canonical model:

1. commitment
2. first proof
3. identity deepening
4. proof emergence
5. alumni compounding
6. new penetration fuel
7. new audience entry

Then the loop repeats.

---

## Stage Definitions

### 1. Commitment

The learner joins or commits.

Risk:

- commitment without confidence

Key job:

- establish trust quickly

### 2. First Proof

The learner experiences an early visible win.

Risk:

- no meaningful proof before doubt returns

Key job:

- reduce the value realization gap

### 3. Identity Deepening

The learner starts acting from the new identity, not just experimenting with a tool.

Examples:

- uses AI with more intentionality
- explains work more clearly
- can defend outputs
- shows authorship

### 4. Proof Emergence

The transformation becomes visible enough to package.

Examples:

- artifact
- before/after story
- public share
- improved decision story
- teacher / parent / peer recognition

### 5. Alumni Compounding

Former participants continue producing trust and access beyond the original program cycle.

Examples:

- alumni referrals
- alumni introductions to schools or communities
- alumni proof stories
- alumni as bridge nodes

### 6. New Penetration Fuel

The proof objects and alumni signals become top-of-funnel assets.

Examples:

- landing-page proof
- short-form social proof
- school outreach proof pack
- creator collaboration proof

### 7. New Audience Entry

Those assets make new people enter with less resistance because:

- the category is clearer
- the product feels more real
- the trust burden is lower

New audience entry is still gated by:

- Phase 0 Product Truth for that audience
- direct evidence that the student loop is repeatable
- Trevor approval before promotion from hypothesis/researching to active

---

## What Deeper Adoption Should Produce

Retention should not only reduce churn.
It should create outputs the rest of the system can use.

### Required outputs

- stronger artifact completion
- stronger before/after narratives
- stronger shareable moments
- stronger alumni participation
- stronger introductions into adjacent clusters
- proof that can be translated into content briefs, friction fixes, and referral assets

If retention does not produce those things, expansion will stay weak.

---

## Proof Emergence Model

K2M should deliberately design for proof to emerge, not hope it happens.

### Proof classes

- identity proof
- outcome proof
- artifact proof
- process proof
- social proof

### Examples

- "I used to freeze when thinking about what comes next"
- "I can now use AI to make decisions with more clarity"
- a finished artifact
- a public reflection post
- a teacher / parent / peer validation moment

---

## Alumni Compounding Model

Alumni are not just past customers.
They are active market nodes.

### Alumni jobs inside the system

- proof carriers
- trust carriers
- bridge nodes
- community stabilizers
- referral sources
- school and workplace introducers

### Compounding rule

The goal is not maximum alumni volume.
It is useful alumni density in relevant clusters.

---

## How Retention Creates New Penetration Fuel

The relationship should be explicit:

| Retention win | Expansion output | Penetration use |
|---|---|---|
| strong first-value moment | early testimony | top-of-funnel reassurance |
| strong artifact completion | portfolio-ready proof | landing pages and outreach |
| strong community belonging | alumni participation | referral and bridge-building |
| strong identity deepening | clear before/after story | category belief and product belief proof |
| strong continued engagement | adjacent-cluster introduction | new beachhead access |

The loop is not closed until the proof actually changes a live penetration asset or routing plan.

## Expansion Metrics

Track expansion separately from referral volume.

### Core metrics

- artifact completion rate
- public-share rate
- alumni response rate
- alumni introduction rate
- proof asset reuse rate
- adjacent-cluster entry rate
- expansion-sourced leads
- expansion-sourced conversions
- cohort application rate uplift from proof-led content
- DM reply rate uplift from proof-led outreach

### Warning metrics

- high retention but low proof output
- high satisfaction but low alumni participation
- many shares but no weak-tie crossing
- many referrals from the same cluster only

## Expansion Workflows

### Workflow 1: Proof Capture

1. identify transformation moment
2. capture proof
3. review for clarity and consent
4. package as reusable asset
5. route into penetration and sales
6. test whether the asset improves external metrics

### Workflow 2: Alumni Activation

1. identify high-signal alumni
2. map their cluster relevance
3. offer a specific advocacy or introduction path
4. capture resulting routes and outcomes
5. record whether new bridge access or content proof emerged

### Workflow 3: Adjacent Cluster Entry

1. identify promising adjacent cluster
2. find alumni or proof bridge
3. adapt proof to the new audience
4. test entry
5. review whether new penetration actually occurred
6. do not activate the new audience without Phase 0 truth and approval

## Implementation State

Current state in K2M:

- the student audience is the only active implementation slice
- teacher, principal, and B2B expansion remain hypothesis or researching states
- the architecture for expansion is defined, but the full expansion loop is **NOT YET IMPLEMENTED** as a repeatable operating release

---

## Minimum Viable Expansion System

If K2M wants the smallest useful version, start with:

1. artifact completion tracking
2. alumni proof capture
3. one structured alumni referral / introduction path
4. one adjacent-cluster entry experiment per cycle
5. direct measurement of whether proof-fed assets improve application or DM response

That is enough to make expansion visible.

---

## Canonical Rule

Retention is not complete when a learner stays.
Retention is complete when deeper adoption produces proof the market can feel.

That proof is the beginning of expansion.
