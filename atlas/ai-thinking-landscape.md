# AI Thinking Landscape

> *The emerging territory of "how to think with AI" — who's building it, who's talking about it, and where the gaps are.*

## Definition

The space where AI cognition meets human cognition. Not "how to use AI tools" but "how to think *with* AI" — using AI as a [[predictive-processing|generative model]] expander, a belief-revision instrument, and a cognitive partnership.

## Key Concepts in the Space

- **Intelligent Interrogation** (Harvard Business Review) — the skill of thinking with AI to produce measurably better outcomes. Not asking better questions — thinking *through* the interaction.
- **Cognitive Partnership** — AI as thinking partner, not tool. The shift from "AI does X for me" to "AI helps me see what I can't see alone."
- **Meta-prompting** — asking nuanced, iterative, strategic questions to guide AI and amplify thinking. The quality of output depends on the quality of the cognitive loop.
- **Prompt Engineering as Epistemology** — the philosophical framing: prompt crafting is a form of knowledge creation. How you frame the question determines what knowledge becomes possible.
- **AI as Thinking Partner, Not Answer Machine** — educational implementation. Students collaborate with AI to deepen thinking, not to outsource it.

## Key Players

### Academic/Institutional
- **Stanford Teaching Commons** — AI literacy framework for education
- **AI Literacy Framework Org** — global framework, 2026 release
- **AI Literacy Institute** — reviews, global surveys on AI skills
- **Harvard Business Review** — "intelligent interrogation" skill
- **US Dept of Labor** — AI literacy for workforce

### Thinkers
- **Sander Schulhoff** — Learn Prompting, HackAPrompt. First prompt engineering guide on the internet.
- **Tim Dasey** — argues AI literacy frameworks are missing giant swaths of relevant knowledge
- **Anil Clifford** — "Thinking WITH AI" vs "Using AI" framing

### Africa/Kenya-Specific
- **Kytabu** (Tonee Ndungu) — AI-powered learning tools for African curriculum, founded 2012
- **M-Shule** — SMS-based AI learning for students without smartphones
- **Generation Kenya** — upskills marginalized youth. Behavior + belief + skills model (closest to our approach)

### Broader Tools
- **OpenAI/ChatGPT** — dominant, most users treat as answer machine
- **Claude/Anthropic** — positioning as "thinking partner"
- **NotebookLM (Google)** — AI for structured research and thinking
- **Cursor/Copilot** — AI-augmented coding as thinking

## The Gap Nobody Is Filling

1. **Belief Operations for Students** — nobody teaches students *how their beliefs work*, let alone how to use AI for [[agm-belief-revision|belief revision]]
2. **AI as Belief-Update Tool** — everyone says "productivity" or "learning." Nobody says "use AI to surface, examine, and update limiting beliefs"
3. **Africa-Designed Frameworks** — global frameworks (Stanford, DOL) are Western-designed. Kenya-specific AI literacy that respects cultural belief systems doesn't exist at scale
4. **Cartographic Approach** — nobody treats knowledge as territory to be mapped

## Our Position

We build at the intersection of:

- [[kenyan-student-belief-artifacts|Kenyan student context]] (specific artifacts, institutions, cultural beliefs)

## Diagnostic Question

*Am I using AI to confirm what I already believe, or to discover what I'm wrong about?*

## Trap

Using AI as an answer machine is the same trap as using the 8-4-4 system to produce scores — it bypasses [[beginners-mind|the actual thinking]] and produces [[goodhart|optimized outputs that miss the point]].
