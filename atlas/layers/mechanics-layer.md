# The Mechanics Layer (Layer 2)

> *What apprentices learn. 15% of expertise. Techniques, methods, procedures.*

## Definition

The techniques, methods, procedures, and tools of a field. The "how to do X" knowledge.

**Examples:**
- Knife skills, chord progressions, design patterns
- Recipes, formulas, frameworks
- Best practices, standard procedures
- Step-by-step guides, tutorials

## Characteristics

- Teachable, codifiable, transferable
- **15% of expertise**
- What schools teach, what books document
- What courses deliver

## The Trap

> *"Mechanics without understanding creates technicians, not artists."*

Knowing *how* backpropagation works without knowing *why* it's the right answer to the credit assignment problem. Applying techniques without understanding which structural force the technique is serving.

## The Transition

The first failure that doesn't have an obvious fix. The model that "should work" but doesn't. The tutorial that breaks when the data changes. Cargo-culting stops being sufficient.
