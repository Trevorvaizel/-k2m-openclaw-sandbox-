## Role
Derivation, evidence, and lens support. This transcript set explains the marketing/belief operations territory through conversational expansion, examples, and repeated framing. It also identifies *The Great Mental Models* as a lens layer that helps the user see the manifesto clearly. It should feed the atlas through source-grounded concepts, diagnostics, lens notes, and case patterns, not by becoming canon wholesale.

## New Knowledge
- The market map is not the market; field contact and behavioral evidence should correct the model.
- Marketing works as a belief update operation, not just persuasion, content production, or lead capture.
- Trust, friction, identity stake, and timing determine whether a belief update can move.
- Defining moments can alter belief systems faster than ordinary messaging when the audience is already near a threshold.
- Internal resistance is part of the trust architecture, not merely objection handling.
- Population-level belief movement depends on network architecture and repeated proof objects.
- Mental models can function as lenses: they clear perception before the belief-operations lattice connects the manifesto to action.
