# Representation Force

> What you can solve depends on what you can represent.

## Definition
The representation force is the way diagrams, models, language, dashboards, ontologies, and maps act as perceptual prosthetics that either reveal or compress away the variables that matter.

## Pattern
- teams stay trapped when their categories are too crude for the real problem
- representation is not mere reporting; it changes what becomes thinkable
- better maps often unlock better interventions before any system change occurs
- changing the representation can be higher leverage than arguing over conclusions

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto names diagrams, simulations, user journeys, forecast tables, and ontologies as representations that can either expose hidden structure or hide decisive variables.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What representation is shaping our judgment right now?
- Which variable disappears inside this model?
- What would a better map make obvious?
- Are we solving the problem poorly because we are seeing it poorly?

## Trap or Limitation
Confusing prettier representation with stronger representation. The question is not elegance but whether decisive structure becomes legible.
