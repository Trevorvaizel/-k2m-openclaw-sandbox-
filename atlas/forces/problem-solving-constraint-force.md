# Constraint Force

> Motion without throughput usually means the governing constraint is untouched.

## Definition
The constraint force is the reality that every system has a limiting factor, and most improvement efforts fail because they optimize locally while leaving that limiter in place.

## Pattern
- organizations improve everywhere instead of where it matters
- activity gets rewarded even when throughput does not change
- the real bottleneck may be a capability, decision right, trust choke point, or fear rather than a machine or a budget line
- until the constraint is named, most improvement is theater

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto points to overloaded reviewers, blocked decision rights, shared dependencies, and hidden fear as common forms of the actual constraint.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What is limiting throughput right now?
- What would remain broken even if all local teams improved?
- Is the bottleneck material, procedural, political, or trust-based?
- Are we celebrating motion while the governing constraint persists?

## Trap or Limitation
Confusing a loud local issue with the real system constraint. Not every pain point is the limiter.
