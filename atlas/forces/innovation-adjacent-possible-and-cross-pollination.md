# Adjacent Possible and Cross-Pollination

> Breakthroughs usually look like combinations before they look like inventions.

## Definition
This force describes how innovation emerges when technologies, costs, interfaces, and behaviors become combinable in a way that was impossible one step earlier, especially for builders positioned at productive intersections.

## Pattern
- many breakthroughs are convergence events
- domain depth plus cross-domain breadth makes combinations visible
- the frontier is structured by newly combinable curves, not random genius alone
- fresh eyes at boundaries often see what insiders dismiss

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto pulls together Steven Johnson, Frans Johansson, and Kauffman's adjacent-possible frame to argue that "visionary" innovation is often a well-timed combination event.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- Which enabling curves just crossed?
- What became buildable one step ago?
- Which adjacent domains need to collide for this to exist?

## Trap or Limitation
Confusing novelty with adjacency. Productive combination requires real enabling change, not arbitrary mashups.
