# Automation Compression and Taste Scarcity

> As execution gets cheaper, differentiated judgment becomes more economically important.

## Definition
Automation compression and taste scarcity describe the double movement created by AI: supply expands as production costs fall, while judgment, taste, trust, proprietary context, and distribution become scarcer and more valuable.

## Pattern
- AI lowers execution cost across software, media, services, and research
- generic output becomes cheaper and more abundant
- differentiation shifts toward selection, taste, trust, context, and access to demand
- execution alone stops being a durable moat

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto treats software prototyping, copy, support, media editing, merchandising, code shipping, and research assistance as examples of compressed execution, then argues that judgment, taste, strategic selection, and proprietary context rise in economic importance.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What becomes commoditized if execution gets cheaper?
- What part of this offer still depends on judgment, trust, or proprietary context?
- Are we competing on production volume when the real bottleneck is taste or distribution?

## Trap or Limitation
Reading AI compression as total replacement. The more accurate reading is that generic execution cheapens while non-generic judgment hardens in value.
