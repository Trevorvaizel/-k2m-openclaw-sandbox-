# Asymmetric Search And Optionality

> Innovation is a portfolio search problem under uncertainty.

## Definition
The asymmetric-search-and-optionality force is the practice of buying information cheaply, preserving upside, and structuring downside so discovery can continue without brittle overcommitment.

## Pattern
- uncertainty is managed through bounded experiments, not giant certain bets
- optionality keeps recombination and re-aiming alive
- good innovation preserves the ability to pivot until conviction is earned
- premature commitment often destroys the search before the search space clarifies

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto connects lean loops, staged capital, discovery-driven planning, and rapid prototyping to the deeper principle of preserving optionality under uncertainty.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What information are we buying with this experiment?
- What downside have we made survivable?
- What option are we killing too early?
- Are we acting as though conviction exists before the search has matured?

## Trap or Limitation
Using optionality to avoid commitment indefinitely. The point is to preserve choice until evidence justifies focus, not to remain permanently diffuse.
