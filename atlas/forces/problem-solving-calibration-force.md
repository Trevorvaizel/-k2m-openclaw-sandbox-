# Calibration Force

> Hard problems become harder when confidence is not accountable to outcomes.

## Definition
The calibration force is the requirement to express uncertainty in ways that can later be checked, so overconfidence and under-confidence stop corrupting diagnosis and action.

## Pattern
- vague certainty hides learning failure
- ranges, probabilities, and reference classes discipline judgment
- confidence should be scored against outcomes rather than protected by charisma
- calibration is a structural property of a team, not just a personality trait

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto draws on superforecasting, heuristics research, and bounded rationality to argue that explicit uncertainty language improves judgment quality.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- How confident are we, in checkable terms?
- What range or probability would we actually attach to this judgment?
- What would count as being wrong?
- Is certainty being used as status rather than evidence?

## Trap or Limitation
Performing precision theatrically. False decimal confidence is still overconfidence.
