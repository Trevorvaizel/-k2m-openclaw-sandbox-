# Attention Gate

> Nothing happens until attention opens.

## Definition
The attention gate is the force by which a story earns the right to continue by disturbing a pattern, opening a meaningful question, or implying consequence quickly enough for attention to stay.

## Pattern
- openings are interruption problems before they are information problems
- attention opens when a pattern is disturbed or a human stake becomes legible
- stories lose force when they explain before they earn notice
- a weak opening is often a force failure, not merely a sentence-level problem

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto treats the first sentence, image, beat, cut, or utterance as the moment where attention either opens or the story fails to begin in the audience's mind.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What disturbance opens the story?
- What question or consequence appears early enough to matter?
- Is the opening making a promise or only delivering information?
- Where does attention close?

## Trap or Limitation
Confusing shock with attention. Interruption without consequence creates momentary notice but weak carry.
