# Outcome Discipline

> Outputs are seductive because they are easy to count. Outcomes are difficult because they force causality.

## Definition
The outcome-discipline force is the requirement to judge product work by changed user and business reality rather than feature throughput or dashboard theater.

## Pattern
- outputs look productive even when the business remains fragile
- metrics can create cleaner hiding places for bad judgment
- AI-era vanity metrics often overstate value while understating durable change
- outcomes force teams to ask whether they actually changed reality

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto contrasts shipping ten features with improving activation, retention, expansion, or willingness to pay, and warns that green dashboards can coexist with fragile businesses.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What user or business reality changed?
- What metric actually corresponds to value?
- Are we measuring movement or movement that matters?
- What would count as outcome-level disconfirmation?

## Trap or Limitation
Treating outcomes as self-evident. Outcomes still require judgment about what value really is.
