# Status And Desire Force

> Every scene is social, even when the social field is only imagined.

## Definition
The status-and-desire force is the movement of leverage, wanting, fear, and social position that gives a scene bloodstream.

## Pattern
- scenes die when nothing shifts in status, leverage, or desire
- even private scenes carry imagined social consequences
- the audience tracks what people want and what it costs to move
- desire without pressure stays inert

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto frames scene life around who wants what, who has leverage, and what the move will cost.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- Who wants what here?
- Who currently has leverage?
- What status shift occurs?
- What becomes more expensive as the scene progresses?

## Trap or Limitation
Reducing every scene to dominance games. Status is one current in the scene, not the whole sea.
