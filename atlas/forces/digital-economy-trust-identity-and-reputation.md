# Trust, Identity, And Reputation

> In digital environments, trust must often be earned without physical proximity.

## Definition
The trust-identity-and-reputation force is the way reputational systems, identity systems, history, reviews, brand memory, and accumulated proof lower economic friction across digital transactions.

## Pattern
- trust becomes infrastructure when proximity disappears
- reputation changes click-through, conversion, ranking, retention, and partnership terms
- where trust accumulates, acquisition gets cheaper
- where trust collapses, friction explodes

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto treats identity systems, review systems, creator brands, domain reputation, and customer history as the trust substrate of digital commerce.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What reputational asset is carrying this transaction?
- Where does the buyer get confidence without proximity?
- Which trust signal is rented and which is portable?
- What happens economically if the trust layer breaks?

## Trap or Limitation
Mistaking visibility for trust. Reach can accelerate a weak trust system, but it cannot replace one.
