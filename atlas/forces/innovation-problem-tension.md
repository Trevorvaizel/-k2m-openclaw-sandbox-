# Problem Tension

> Innovation begins in unresolved demand, not in technology.

## Definition
Problem tension is the structural pressure created by a job, contradiction, delay, or impossibility already present in the world before the builder falls in love with a solution.

## Pattern
- capabilities are seductive before demand is legible
- markets hire progress, not novelty
- whoever names the demand most accurately often owns the framing layer
- the problem is usually more valuable than the first solution

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto brings Christensen and Blank into the same law: innovation fails when it starts from capability worship instead of unresolved demand contact.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What tension already exists in the world?
- What workaround cost are people already paying?
- Are we in love with the capability or in contact with the demand?

## Trap or Limitation
Treating every pain point as innovation-worthy. Real tension matters when it is economically, socially, or operationally consequential.
