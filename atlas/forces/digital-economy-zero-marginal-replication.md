# Zero-Marginal Replication

> Digital labor can sometimes be converted into assets that detach output from hourly effort.

## Definition
Zero-marginal replication is the force by which software, media libraries, templates, APIs, recordings, and automations can be created once and distributed many times at negligible additional cost.

## Pattern
- the first unit is expensive
- subsequent copies are cheap
- margins improve when output detaches from labor-time
- the force rewards asset creation over endless custom repetition

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto groups software margins, media libraries, recorded knowledge, reusable automation, and templates under one law: digital goods can scale economically because copying them is structurally cheap.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- Is this work still trapped in hourly substitution?
- What part of this service can become an asset?
- What becomes cheaper to distribute after the first build?

## Trap or Limitation
Cheap replication does not guarantee demand. The asset still needs distribution, trust, and a viable market position.
