# Geometry Force (Technologist Force 1)

> *Learning is the transformation of space. Every model is a machine that reshapes a geometric world.*

## The Surface Pattern
When a model is trained, numbers change. Parameters update. Loss decreases.

## The Invisible Mechanism
Data exists in high-dimensional space. A ML model is a **sequence of geometric transformations** â€” each layer stretches, rotates, and reshapes the space. Training searches for the transformation sequence that arranges space so things that belong together are near each other.

## The Mathematical Grammar
- **Vectors** = points in space
- **Matrices** = transformations of space
- **Dot products** = alignment (why they measure similarity)
- **Eigenvectors** = natural axes of a transformation (why PCA works)
- **Gradients** = directions in parameter space
- **Entropy** = volume of uncertain space

## The Second-Order Force
Architecture encodes geometric assumptions. CNNs assume spatial locality and translation invariance. Transformers assume any element can be relevant to any other. **The architecture that wins is the one whose geometric assumptions align with the structure of the domain.**

Depth is powerful because each layer adds another transformation building more abstract structure. Deep learning = hierarchical geometry.

## The Application
> *Every great ML paper, at its core, is a claim about geometry.*

## Cross-Domain Connection
This is [[asymmetric-leverage]] in the AI domain: the geometric primitives are the leverage points. Understanding them makes every architecture legible.
