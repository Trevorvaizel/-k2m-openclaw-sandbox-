# Collective Intelligence Force

> Hard problems often require superminds, not soloists.

## Definition
The collective intelligence force is the reality that some problems exceed individual cognition and become solvable only when humans, roles, and tools are arranged into a better thinking system.

## Pattern
- no single perspective can hold the whole problem
- group intelligence rises or collapses based on coordination design
- decision quality depends on how perspectives, models, and feedback are combined
- human-AI systems change the shape of what can be decomposed, simulated, and checked

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto places Thomas Malone and frontier human-AI problem solving near the leading edge of the field, arguing that the future is moving toward human-AI systems rather than lone-genius heroics.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What intelligence is missing from the room?
- Is this problem being assigned to an individual that needs a system?
- How are disagreement, synthesis, and feedback structured?
- What part should machines assist without becoming false authority?

## Trap or Limitation
Assuming more people automatically means more intelligence. Poorly designed collaboration can deepen noise, politics, and diffusion of responsibility.
