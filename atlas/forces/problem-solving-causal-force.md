# Causal Force

> Symptoms are not causes. Explanation is not intervention.

## Definition
The causal force is the discipline of distinguishing visible correlation from real mechanism and translating a story about what happened into a testable intervention logic.

## Pattern
- most teams can explain after the fact but cannot specify what would change the outcome
- causal claims often hide untested counterfactuals
- interventions fail when the team never defined what evidence would falsify its story
- stronger causal thinking reduces symptom management disguised as rigor

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto centers questions like "What would happen if we intervened here?" and "What alternative cause could generate the same visible pattern?"

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What mechanism are we claiming?
- What intervention would test that claim?
- What evidence would falsify our causal story?
- What other cause could generate the same pattern?

## Trap or Limitation
Mistaking elegant explanation for validated causality. A coherent narrative is still only a hypothesis.
