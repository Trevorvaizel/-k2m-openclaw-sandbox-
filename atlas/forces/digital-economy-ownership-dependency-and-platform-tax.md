# Ownership, Dependency, and Platform Tax

> Not all access is ownership.

## Definition
Ownership, dependency, and platform tax describe the unequal economic strength of rented reach, portable relationship assets, and sovereign embedded assets, along with the margin loss imposed by platform-controlled surfaces.

## Pattern
- followers and impressions are rented reach
- direct payment relationships, first-party data, and customer history are more portable
- embedded brand memory, domains, proprietary data, and operational systems are more sovereign
- platform fees and referral dependence extract value from weak ownership positions

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto explicitly ranks rented reach, portable relationship assets, and embedded sovereign assets as different tiers, then ties app-store fees, marketplace take rates, ad dependency, and AI summarization risk to the cost of weak ownership.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What do we actually own here?
- Which asset would survive if the platform changed policy?
- Where is the platform taxing margin, access, or customer relationship?

## Trap or Limitation
Confusing reach with durable asset strength. A large rented audience can be weaker than a smaller direct customer base.
