# Organizational Antibodies And Ambidexterity

> Organizations kill more innovations than markets do.

## Definition
The organizational-antibodies-and-ambidexterity force is the tendency of existing incentives, metrics, reporting lines, and identity commitments to reject tomorrow's logic in order to protect today's operating system.

## Pattern
- incumbent structures favor the already-working system
- disruptive possibilities initially look worse by current metrics
- new logic needs political shelter before reality can defend it
- ambidexterity is not a slogan but a structural requirement

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto connects Christensen, Bahcall, Govindarajan, and O'Reilly/Tushman around the need for protected space where new logic can survive until it proves itself.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- Which metric makes the new logic look worse than it is?
- Where are the antibodies located: budget, reporting line, success definition, or identity?
- What protected space exists for the new logic?
- Are we asking a legacy system to judge a future system by legacy criteria?

## Trap or Limitation
Romanticizing every rejected idea as disruption. Some projects fail because they are weak, not because antibodies attacked them.
