# Auction Force

> Priced attention flows toward expected value, not human importance.

## Definition
The auction force is the pull exerted by monetized attention systems toward actors with high margins, strong data, high expected conversion value, and enough bidding power to buy or influence visibility.

## Pattern
- commercial value shapes who can buy attention
- platform scoring and expected return influence allocation
- public goods often cannot bid back
- monetized attention is a market-design problem, not just an ad problem

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto argues that attention markets do not naturally reward truth, learning, dignity, or civic patience. They reward measurable expected return, which is why structurally valuable but weakly monetized outcomes get crowded out.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- Who can afford to buy this attention?
- Which outcomes monetize poorly but matter deeply?
- Does the scoring system account for user welfare or only predicted action?
- What gets crowded out by better margins and data?

## Trap or Limitation
Treating monetization as neutral plumbing. Auctions quietly reweight the field toward actors with higher expected value, not necessarily higher social value.
