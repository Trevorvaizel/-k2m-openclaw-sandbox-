# Salience Force

> What becomes noticeable becomes possible.

## Definition
The salience force is the power to shape what enters consideration by controlling contrast, position, motion, repetition, urgency, personalization, and perceptual hierarchy.

## Pattern
- people do not scan reality democratically
- the first, largest, moving, repeated, or urgent thing gets disproportionate consideration
- salience becomes institutional power when tied to ranking, money, or routing
- "the user chose it" is incomplete when choice happens inside a designed perceptual field

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto moves from interface details such as badges and preselected options to system-scale salience like ranked results, promoted listings, AI answers, and feed recommendations.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What is first?
- What is largest or moving?
- What is technically available but perceptually buried?
- Is salience answerable to the user's purpose?

## Trap or Limitation
Trying to remove salience entirely. The deeper question is whether salience serves the user or exploits their perceptual vulnerability.
