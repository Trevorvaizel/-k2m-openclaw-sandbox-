# Ranking, Recommendation, And Feedback Loops

> Ranking systems silently allocate opportunity.

## Definition
The ranking-recommendation-and-feedback-loops force is the way search, feed, marketplace, app-store, and AI citation systems use interaction data to determine visibility, which then compounds further interaction and further ranking advantage.

## Pattern
- better placement creates more interaction data
- more interaction data improves future prediction
- rankings often become decisive before participants understand the allocation logic
- visibility, installs, clicks, and seller success can all be downstream of ranking engines

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto describes how search, feed, marketplace, app-store, and AI citation ranking decide who gets seen, clicked, installed, or allowed to compound.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- Which ranking engine is allocating this opportunity?
- What signal does it reward?
- What interaction loop makes the winner harder to displace?
- What visibility is being mistaken for intrinsic merit?

## Trap or Limitation
Treating ranking logic as everything. It is powerful, but it still sits inside broader trust, demand, and ownership structures.
