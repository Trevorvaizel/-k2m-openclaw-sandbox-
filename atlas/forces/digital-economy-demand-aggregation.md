# Demand Aggregation

> The player who aggregates demand usually gains structural power over fragmented suppliers.

## Definition
Demand aggregation is the force by which search engines, feeds, marketplaces, app stores, and discovery surfaces accumulate user starting points and convert that position into bargaining power.

## Pattern
- users begin the journey in one place
- suppliers become dependent on access to that place
- discovery control turns into rent-extraction power
- scale matters because it concentrates buyer intent, not only traffic

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto treats search engines, app stores, marketplaces, social feeds, video platforms, and AI discovery surfaces as variants of the same structural position: once the demand journey starts there, upstream creators and merchants negotiate from dependency.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- Where does customer intent start?
- Who owns the default discovery surface?
- If access to that surface changed tomorrow, how much bargaining power disappears?

## Trap or Limitation
Confusing distribution volume with aggregated demand. A big audience is weaker than a surface where users begin their search or transaction.
