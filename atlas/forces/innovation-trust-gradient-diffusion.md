# Trust-Gradient Diffusion

> Innovation spreads socially before it spreads economically.

## Definition
Trust-gradient diffusion is the force by which new categories move through bridges, opinion leaders, threshold bands, and layered trust sequences rather than through abstract demand alone.

## Pattern
- adoption is carried socially before it is normalized economically
- category creation depends on trust transport
- the question is not only who the customer is, but which nodes move the belief into the next cluster
- identity and integrity become decisive as adoption broadens

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto fuses Rogers, Moore, Granovetter, and K2M's trust gradient into one law: adoption sequences through awareness, credibility, competence, integrity, and identity instead of happening all at once.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- Which nodes can move this belief into the next cluster?
- What trust layer is actually missing?
- Are we asking for identity commitment with only awareness-level proof?

## Trap or Limitation
Assuming a superior product will automatically diffuse. Without trust transport, category truth dies locally.
