# Anti-Mimicry

> Form without territory contact produces decorative authority: the reader feels oriented without gaining any new diagnostic power.

## Definition
Anti-mimicry is the force that resists copying the visible shape of prior canon when the underlying structure has not yet been earned.

## Pattern
- templates accelerate drafting
- templates also hide when the map could survive a noun swap
- the stronger the inherited form, the stronger the need for a transplant test
- a section that travels unchanged across domains is usually still too generic

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto names the failure directly: a draft can have preamble, forces, stages, toolkit, and creed, yet still be portable to another field with trivial swaps. That is not transfer. It is mimicry.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- Could this section move to another domain unchanged?
- What would break if I swapped the nouns?
- Did the territory force this structure, or did the template force it?

## Trap or Limitation
Rejecting all inheritance is another error. Transfer is legitimate when the deeper mechanism survives and the mapping tier is named honestly.
