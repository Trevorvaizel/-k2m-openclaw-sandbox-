# Statesman Force 2: TIME COMPRESSION (Political Capital Half-Life)

> The Non-Linearity of Political Influence

## Definition
The Non-Linearity of Political Influence

## Pattern
- Political capital does NOT accumulate linearly. It has half-lives and compound effects.
- Fresh mandates have ~18-month peak effectiveness
- Media attention has ~6-month half-life before novelty fades
- Crisis capital must be converted within ~4 weeks
- Goodwill decays exponentially; resentment compounds linearly
- Political novelty ("youngest MP") has ~12-month shelf life
- You're not building "more capital"
- You're building reputation lattice - a connected network of trust, competence perception, and relationship depth
- Each positive interaction connects to every other one
