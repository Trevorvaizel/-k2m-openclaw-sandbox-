# Native Causality

> A field is not mapped when it is forced to speak borrowed language that cannot explain its own success, failure, trust, or coordination logic.

## Definition
Native causality is the discipline of naming what actually generates value and breakdown inside the field being mapped, rather than importing the favorite abstractions of another canon.

## Pattern
- borrowed concepts can illuminate a domain
- borrowed causality can colonize it
- the wider the title, the more provinces a force must survive
- a force that only fits one province should be narrowed or renamed

## Source-Grounded Example
The Domain Cartographer's Manifesto warns against exemplar cosplay: a document can sound like prior canon while failing to diagnose the new field. The test is whether the force survives across multiple provinces without collapsing into noun-swapping.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What generates value, failure, trust, and legitimacy in this field on its own terms?
- Which of my force names came from the territory, and which came from a manifesto I already liked?
- If this force fails outside one province, should it be narrowed?

## Trap or Limitation
Native language is not enough by itself. A field can describe itself in its own words and still remain blind to its deeper structure.
