# Positioning Gravity

> No product is judged in a vacuum.

## Definition
The positioning-gravity force is the pull exerted by category assumptions, buyer language, alternatives, and perceived status-quo risk on how a product is understood.

## Pattern
- weak framing can hide strong capability
- products are judged inside a comparison set whether the team defines it or not
- commoditized capability increases the importance of workflow fit and category clarity
- many failures are failures of frame, not raw product weakness

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto argues that many products fail not because they are weak, but because they are seen from the wrong frame.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What category frame are buyers using?
- Which alternatives are we being compared against?
- What specific audience can perceive the differentiated value?
- Is the product weak, or merely misframed?

## Trap or Limitation
Treating positioning as copy polish. It is structural product work, not garnish.
