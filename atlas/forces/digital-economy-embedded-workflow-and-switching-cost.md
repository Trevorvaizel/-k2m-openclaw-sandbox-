# Embedded Workflow and Switching Cost

> When software becomes load-bearing in another person's process, it stops behaving like a tool and starts behaving like infrastructure.

## Definition
Embedded workflow and switching cost describe the defensive power gained when a digital product becomes part of recurring process, habit, permissions, data history, and team behavior.

## Pattern
- the product enters a recurring workflow
- surrounding habits and history accumulate around it
- removal creates operational pain even when alternatives look better
- unglamorous infrastructure often has stronger economics than flashy surface tools

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto frames payroll software, accounting systems, payment stacks, project tools, creator workflows, and developer infrastructure as variants of the same force: replacement pain matters more than abstract preference.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What pain appears if this product is removed?
- Is the product liked, or is it load-bearing?
- What habits, data, permissions, or workflows now depend on it?

## Trap or Limitation
Mistaking habit for embed. A product can be familiar without being economically difficult to replace.
