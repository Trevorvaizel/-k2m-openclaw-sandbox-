# Industrialization Gap

> The distance from proof to scaled reality is where frontier innovation goes to die.

## Definition
The industrialization gap is the force separating prototypes, pilots, demos, and papers from dependable, economic, repeatable systems in the world.

## Pattern
- prototype truth is not industrial truth
- manufacturing, safety, deployment, supply chains, regulation, and operator trust are part of the innovation
- many teams can prove something once; far fewer can make it durable
- the gap kills frontier work after the celebratory moment

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto compresses the point into contrasts: a model is not a workflow, a molecule is not a medicine, a drill test is not a power plant, and a robot demo is not industrial deployment.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What has been proven only once?
- What operational, regulatory, manufacturing, or trust layer still separates this from scaled reality?
- What would real repeatability require?

## Trap or Limitation
Calling implementation an afterthought. In frontier innovation, industrialization is part of the innovation itself.
