# Friction Force

> Every path has a cost. Design decides who pays it.

## Definition
The friction force is the behavioral pricing built into every step, delay, ambiguity, buried setting, comparison path, reset flow, and exit route in an attention system.

## Pattern
- friction can protect or extract
- asymmetry between entering and leaving is a governance decision
- interfaces silently legislate behavior through choice cost
- the cost of refusal often matters more than the ease of acceptance

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto frames friction asymmetry as private governance and asks whether leaving is as easy as entering, declining as easy as accepting, and resetting as easy as training the system.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- Is leaving as easy as entering?
- Is declining as easy as accepting?
- Is comparing as easy as buying?
- Is understanding as easy as being acted on?
- Who bears the friction cost?

## Trap or Limitation
Treating all friction as bad. Some friction is protective; the question is whether the asymmetry is justified.
