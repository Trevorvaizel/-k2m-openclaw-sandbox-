# Scarcity Inversion

> Information abundance does not solve attention. It consumes it.

## Definition
Scarcity inversion is the force by which cheaper information production increases the burden of selection, making attention, filtration, trust, and prioritization more valuable than raw publication.

## Pattern
- more content creates more selection burden
- the scarce resource shifts from message creation to qualified human notice
- filters, curators, recommenders, teachers, and agents gain strategic importance
- generative AI intensifies the inversion by making plausible output cheaper

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto treats every new post, tab, notification, answer surface, and generated summary as a new sorting cost imposed somewhere. The scarce thing is not information; it is the moment in which a human can notice, care, understand, choose, and act.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What selection burden does this system create?
- Who performs the filtering?
- Who bears the cost of sorting?
- What makes this attention request legitimate?

## Trap or Limitation
Treating abundance as value by default. More information can degrade the environment when the system externalizes the cost of selection onto the user.
