# Meaning-Making Force

> The audience is not a container. They are a co-authoring intelligence.

## Definition
The meaning-making force is the audience's active construction of causality, motive, theme, and significance inside the ambiguity a storyteller leaves behind.

## Pattern
- audiences infer by default
- meaning is assembled, not installed whole
- too much explanation reduces co-creation
- too little guidance leaves interpretation scattered rather than directed

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto says the storyteller's job is not to install meaning whole but to create conditions under which the audience assembles it in the intended direction.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What meaning is the audience likely to assemble here?
- What ambiguity is productive and what ambiguity is just confusion?
- Are we over-explaining the theme?
- Where is the audience doing the real interpretive work?

## Trap or Limitation
Hiding behind ambiguity. Meaning-making is not abdication; it still requires directional design.
