# Outside-View Force

> The inside story gives meaning. The outside view gives odds.

## Definition
The outside-view force is the discipline of correcting emotionally persuasive local narratives with base rates and reference classes from sufficiently similar cases.

## Pattern
- every effort believes itself special
- detail-rich plans often hide optimism rather than reducing it
- the outside view externalizes memory through base rates
- stronger problem solvers use both the inside meaning and the outside odds

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto asks what happened in similar cases and what base rate governs cost, schedule, adoption, and failure for this class of effort.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What is the reference class?
- What do similar cases say about likely cost, schedule, or failure?
- Are we arguing from uniqueness to escape accountability?
- What would the odds say if our story were not allowed to speak first?

## Trap or Limitation
Applying a sloppy reference class. Bad outside views can mislead as much as romantic inside views.
