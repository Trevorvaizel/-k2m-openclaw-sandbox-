# Timing Windows

> Being early and being wrong are often the same event.

## Definition
Timing windows describe the brief alignments of technology readiness, customer readiness, infrastructure maturity, cost curves, regulation, and distribution that make a true idea economically legible.

## Pattern
- the same product can fail, then dominate later
- adoption requires a window, not just a truth
- timing density converts truth into uptake
- innovators must ask "why now?" with specificity

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto ties timing to Moore, McGrath, and Grove: a product can be structurally sound and still fail because the surrounding system is not yet aligned.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- Why now, specifically?
- What changed in cost, culture, interface, infrastructure, or regulation?
- Is the timing dense enough for uptake?

## Trap or Limitation
Treating timeless correctness as enough. In innovation, mistimed truth can behave like error.
