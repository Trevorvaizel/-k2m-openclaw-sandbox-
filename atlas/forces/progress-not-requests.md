# Progress, Not Requests

> Customers do not buy features. They hire progress.

## Definition
The progress-not-requests force is the discipline of reading user requests as surface artifacts and solving for the underlying progress gap instead.

## Pattern
- requests are often proxies for deeper jobs
- teams that ship requests literally often miss the real user struggle
- the strongest product move is the smallest trustworthy path to progress
- request volume can coexist with low usage when the team solved the wrong thing

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto uses a request for calendar integration to illustrate how the deeper job may actually be confidence, follow-through, reduced anxiety, or remembered commitments.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What progress is the user trying to hire?
- What struggle sits underneath the request?
- What smaller move would prove that progress more directly?
- Are we solving the feature or the user's movement?

## Trap or Limitation
Dismissing requests as naive. Requests still carry signal; the error is stopping at their literal form.
