# Question Engine

> Story runs on unanswered questions, not information density.

## Definition
The question engine is the force that keeps an audience moving by giving them a live question with consequence attached.

## Pattern
- information alone does not carry narrative momentum
- a story advances when the audience is carrying a question they need resolved
- stalled stories often reveal a dead or trivial question engine
- better questions create better narrative pressure than more explanation

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto states directly that when stories stall, the question engine is usually dead.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What question is the audience carrying right now?
- Why does that question matter?
- When does the story answer too early or fail to deepen the question?
- Is the audience being made curious or merely informed?

## Trap or Limitation
Stacking mystery without consequence. A question that does not matter still will not carry the audience very far.
