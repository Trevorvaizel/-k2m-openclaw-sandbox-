# Narrative Trust Force

> Every story is an agreement between maker and audience.

## Definition
The narrative trust force is the ongoing judgment an audience makes about whether the storyteller understands the world, knows where the story is going, earns emotion honestly, and deserves continued attention.

## Pattern
- trust is built scene by scene and beat by beat
- the audience keeps asking whether the storyteller is wasting time or cheating emotion
- technical competence without trust produces shallow engagement
- broken trust can make later payoffs feel manipulative even when the structure is correct

## Source-Grounded Example
The manifesto frames audience trust as a continuous test: do you know where this is going, are you wasting my time, are you being honest about this world, are you earning this emotion?

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask:
- What promise has the story made?
- Is the emotion earned or extracted?
- Does the storyteller feel in command of the world and sequence?
- Where does the audience likely feel handled rather than guided?

## Trap or Limitation
Using trust language as a substitute for structural repair. Trust usually breaks because some deeper narrative force was mishandled.
