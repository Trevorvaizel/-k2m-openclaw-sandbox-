# Map Is Not the Territory Lens

> Treat every model as a map: useful because it simplifies, dangerous when mistaken for reality.

## Definition
The map-is-not-the-territory lens separates an abstraction from the living territory it represents. A map can be a report, model, forecast, policy, MOC, or belief map. It earns trust only by staying useful against the territory.

## Pattern
- A model reduces reality so a person can act.
- The reduction hides details, values, context, and change.
- The map is fit to a purpose, not to every possible use of the territory.
- The map carries the cartographer's incentives, viewpoint, and omissions.
- The user forgets the reduction and treats the map as the territory.
- Bad decisions follow because the person optimizes the abstraction instead of the world, or tries to force the world to fit the abstraction.

## Source-Grounded Example
Transcript 00 uses the one-to-one map joke to show why perfect fidelity can become useless, then contrasts Newtonian physics and relativity to show that good maps have valid ranges rather than permanent authority. Transcript 01 deepens the lens with the London Underground map: it is excellent for route connections and misleading for walking distance, which means the first test of a map is the job it was drawn to perform.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "What job was this map built for, who drew it, where does it break, and what field contact would update it?"

Use this lens when a belief map, strategy, report, or mental model starts acting like doctrine.

## Trap
Rejecting maps because they are imperfect. The right lesson is not "all maps are useless"; it is "all maps need purpose, range, and update pressure."
