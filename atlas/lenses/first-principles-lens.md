# First Principles Lens

> Separate the non-reducible facts from inherited assumptions, analogies, and "how it is always done."

## Definition
The first-principles lens decomposes a problem until it reaches fixed realities for the context. It then rebuilds from those realities instead of copying inherited methods.

## Pattern
- The user names a belief, method, or received story.
- They challenge assumptions, evidence, alternatives, consequences, and the original question.
- They keep asking why until they reach a falsifiable fact or a genuine mechanism.
- They separate principle from tactic, and law from trend.
- They rebuild a better method from the underlying reality.

## Source-Grounded Example
Transcript 00 uses the sterile-stomach ulcer case to show how a medical assumption can masquerade as a first principle. Transcript 03 deepens the lens with explicit questioning methods, Temple Grandin's distinction between low-stress handling and the curved chute tactic, Bertrand Russell's turkey problem, and artificial meat as an example of decomposing a product into molecular requirements rather than inherited production methods.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "What here is a law of the territory, what is just a shared belief or inherited method, and what would falsify my current explanation?"

Use this lens when a team is trapped by default logic.

## Trap
Calling any preferred assumption a first principle, or mistaking a historically successful tactic for the principle that made it work. A first principle must survive evidence pressure in the specific context.
