# Circle of Competence Lens

> Know where your map is deep enough to act and where you are the stranger with shallow confidence.

## Definition
The circle-of-competence lens marks the boundary between lived fluency and borrowed surface knowledge. It is a defense against ego, false confidence, and decisions made outside the range of real understanding.

## Pattern
- A person gets enough surface information to feel competent.
- They miss the tacit knowledge, incentives, local rules, and failure modes known by lifers.
- They act outside their circle and take risks they cannot see.
- They may borrow a lifer's judgment without checking the lifer's incentives.
- The repair is curiosity, track-record monitoring, external feedback, and finding true lifers when needed.

## Source-Grounded Example
Transcript 00 introduces the lifer-versus-stranger contrast and Everest as the difference between reading a map and surviving a territory. Transcript 02 deepens the lens with Rose Blumkin's refusal to buy what she did not understand, Atul Gawande's use of a coach to expose blind spots inside an already deep circle, and Queen Elizabeth I's use of structured counsel when she had to govern beyond her own direct expertise.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "Am I a lifer here, or a stranger using borrowed vocabulary? If I must act outside my circle, whose judgment am I borrowing, and how are they paid?"

Use this lens before making high-stakes decisions in unfamiliar territory.

## Trap
Using the circle as an excuse to avoid growth, or assuming that hiring an expert removes the need for judgment. The transcript frames the circle as maintainable and expandable through learning, track records, outside feedback, and incentive-aware use of local guides.
