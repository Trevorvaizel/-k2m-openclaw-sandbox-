# Second-Order Thinking Lens

> Do not stop at the immediate result. Ask what the result will cause next.

## Definition
The second-order thinking lens tracks effects of effects. It asks how an action changes incentives, systems, behavior, and future constraints after the first-order gain appears.

## Pattern
- A quick fix produces an obvious benefit.
- The system adapts to the new incentive or pressure.
- The adaptation consumes or reverses the original benefit.
- The wiser decision accounts for downstream behavior before acting.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript uses the cobra effect to show how a metric-targeting intervention can create the opposite behavior. It also uses livestock antibiotics and Buffett's parade metaphor to show that downstream adaptation and relative positioning can erase the apparent first-order win.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "And then what happens?"

Use this lens when an intervention changes incentives, status, behavior, ecology, or long-term trust.

## Trap
Becoming paralyzed by distant possibilities. The lens should identify likely consequential effects, not invent endless hypothetical doom.
