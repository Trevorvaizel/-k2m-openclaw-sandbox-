# Statesman: THE STATESMAN'S CREED

> The Map Is Not The Territory - All political models are approximations. The master statesman knows their maps are wrong - and uses them anyway.

## Definition
The Map Is Not The Territory - All political models are approximations. The master statesman knows their maps are wrong - and uses them anyway.

## Pattern
- All Mastery Is Self-Discovery - I cannot give you political wisdom. I can only show you the path. Mastery is earned, not transferred.
- The Beginner's Mind Is The Only Mind - The moment you think you've mastered politics, you've started declining. Mastery is not a destination. It's a practice of returning to "I don't know."
- Context Is Everything - There are no universal political rules. There are only context-specific principles. "It depends" is the most accurate answer in existence.
- Time Is Density - Years in politics don't make you better. Density of learning connections does. The thoughtful politician grows faster than the experienced politician.
- Failure Is Data - There is no such thing as a bad political mistake. Only unexamined mistakes. Fail deliberately, fail often, fail forward.
- Mastery Is Service - The highest political mastery serves others, not ego. The true statesman disappears. Only the service remains.
