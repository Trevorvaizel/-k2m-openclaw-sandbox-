# Intelligence: THE CARTOGRAPHER'S CREED

> These are the principles. They do not expire.

## Definition
These are the principles. They do not expire.

## Pattern
- Principle 1: THE MAP IS NOT THE TERRITORY
- All intelligence is approximation. The moment you mistake your current map for reality, you have stopped being a cartographer and become a bureaucrat defending an old chart.
- Hold every map loosely. Update constantly. Never confuse confidence in your reading with certainty about the terrain.
- Principle 2: THE MOST IMPORTANT INTELLIGENCE IS WHAT YOU DON'T WANT TO HEAR
- Every intelligence system gravitates toward comfortable confirmation.
- The cartographer's discipline is to specifically seek the signal that challenges the current map. The uncomfortable source. The contradictory data. The failure that doesn't fit the model.
- *What is this situation telling me that I don't want to believe?*
- This question, asked honestly and regularly, is worth more than any network you can build.
- Principle 3: POSITION IS MORE VALUABLE THAN VOLUME
