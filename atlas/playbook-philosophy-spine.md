# Playbook Philosophy Spine

## Definition

The playbook philosophy spine is the load-bearing logic that keeps the cohort from collapsing into generic AI education. It ties guardrails, jobs-to-be-done, node architecture, and the four habits into one coherent identity-shift model.

## Pattern

Its broad shape is:

- guardrails define what the cohort must never become
- JTBD frames the student's functional, emotional, and social hiring logic
- node architecture turns zone movement into small identity shifts
- the four habits turn those shifts into portable behavioral proof

Together, these make the cohort about thinking transformation rather than tool instruction.

## Diagnostic Use

Ask:

- What has to stay invariant even if the vessel, audience, or tools change?
- Which product decisions protect psychological safety versus merely decorate the curriculum?
- Does a proposed change strengthen identity-shift logic or drift toward shallow AI training?

## Trap

The trap is extracting isolated slogans without preserving the binding logic between guardrails, jobs, nodes, and habits.
