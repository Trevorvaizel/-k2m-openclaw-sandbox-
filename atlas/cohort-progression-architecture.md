# Cohort Progression Architecture

## State

The cohort progression architecture is the eight-week identity sequence that moves a learner from low-trust curiosity to public proof of thinking change.

## Visible Signs

The live progression is:

1. Wonder: AI becomes visible and low-stakes.
2. Trust: small wins and context build confidence.
3. Converse: iteration and dialogue turn AI into a thinking partner.
4. Direct: the learner develops standards, ownership, and direction.
5. Graduation: the artifact and habits make the shift socially legible.

This is more than pacing. Each phase corresponds to a deeper shift in who the student believes they are with AI.

## Transition Trigger

Students progress when the next form of agency becomes believable:

- from "this is not for me" to "I can try this"
- from "it works sometimes" to "context changes the outcome"
- from "AI does tasks" to "we can think together"
- from "AI helps" to "I direct quality and produce proof"

## Trap

The trap is reading the weekly sequence as curriculum choreography only. Its real job is to stage safe identity movement.
