# Attention Design Stage 5: Economic Cartography

> *"Attention is priced, traded, subsidized, and contested."*

## State
The practitioner can map who pays, who profits, who bears cognitive cost, who can exit, who can audit, and which public goods cannot bid back.

## Visible Signs
- reads attention systems as economic allocation systems
- explains why harmless-looking surfaces produce harmful equilibria
- tracks subsidy, profit, and crowd-out rather than only engagement
- sees monetization as market design

## Transition Trigger
Ask: Who is paying for this attention environment, and what kinds of value or harm cannot bid back inside it?

## Trap
Cynicism. The practitioner sees extraction everywhere and forgets that attention can also be designed in service of real human ends.
