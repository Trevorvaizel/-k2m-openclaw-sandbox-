# Stage 6: The Intervention Architect

> "The best solution is a redesigned environment."

## Description
At this stage, the practitioner stops obsessing over isolated events and starts redesigning structures, routines, incentives, and interfaces so whole classes of failure become less likely. They think in learning systems instead of heroic recoveries.

## What Becomes Possible
- shifting from event repair to environment design
- redesigning constraints, feedback, and coordination paths
- treating transition cost and trust as part of the intervention, not afterthoughts

## Blind Spot
Underestimating the emotional and organizational cost of transition.
