# Stage 6: Cross-Medium Cartography

> "I know what belongs to the story and what belongs only to the medium."

## Description
At this stage, the storyteller understands which forces survive translation across formats and which effects are specific to a particular form. They can either translate a narrative across media or exploit one medium with unusual precision.

## What Becomes Possible
- adapting story without losing its underlying engine
- designing around the actual geometry of a medium
- distinguishing durable narrative structure from platform habit

## Blind Spot
Over-control. Medium fluency can tempt the maker into engineering away useful surprise.
