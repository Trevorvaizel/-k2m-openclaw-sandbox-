# Five Whys Practice

> Keep digging until you hit a mechanism instead of a socially acceptable stopping point.

## Purpose
Use the five whys to get below default explanations such as "because that's how we do it" or "because the market is bad." The practice forces a problem down toward root cause.

## Process
- State the visible problem.
- Ask why it is happening.
- For each answer, ask why again.
- Reject bad landings such as authority, inertia, or convention.
- Stop only when you reach a concrete mechanism, a falsifiable fact, or a root constraint you can actually test or change.

## Result
- Surface symptoms stop masquerading as causes.
- Old decisions reveal the hidden assumptions still governing the present.
- The team gets a solvable root problem instead of a ritual complaint.

## When To Use
Use this when a recurring issue keeps getting patched without improving, or when the official explanation sounds too smooth to be true.

## Failure Mode
Stopping at "because we've always done it" or any other culturally convenient answer. That is not a root cause; it is where thinking gave up.
