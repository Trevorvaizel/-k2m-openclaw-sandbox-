# Socratic Questioning Practice

> A disciplined way to strip a confident claim down to the assumptions holding it up.

## Purpose
Use Socratic questioning to separate what you know from what you merely believe. The practice slows down a default narrative and forces explicit contact with evidence, alternatives, consequences, and the real question.

## Process
- Clarify the claim: what exactly am I saying will happen?
- Challenge assumptions: what must be true for this claim to hold?
- Look for evidence: what data supports it, and what is just feeling or borrowed authority?
- Consider alternatives: what would a serious contrary view say?
- Examine consequences: what happens if I am wrong?
- Question the original question: am I solving the right problem at all?

## Result
- Weak assumptions become visible before they become expensive decisions.
- The user gets closer to a falsifiable explanation instead of a persuasive story.
- The practice often changes the question before it changes the answer.

## When To Use
Use this when a decision feels urgent, obvious, or socially reinforced, especially when the downside of being wrong is large.

## Failure Mode
Using the questions as theater instead of pressure. If the answers stay vague, inherited, or untestable, the practice has not reached first principles.
