# Bayesian Update Practice

> Start with the base rate, then let new evidence move the belief by a justified amount.

## Purpose
Use Bayesian updating to avoid swinging from story to story. The practice disciplines first impressions by forcing context, evidence strength, and belief revision into the same frame.

## Process
- State the current claim in probability terms, not certainty terms.
- Identify the prior or base rate before looking at the newest signal.
- Evaluate how strong and relevant the new evidence really is.
- Update the belief by an amount proportional to the evidence, not the vividness of the story.
- Recheck the decision against downside asymmetry and repeat exposure.

## Result
- New information stops hijacking the whole judgment.
- Beliefs move faster when the world has genuinely changed and slower when the signal is weak.
- Forecasting becomes more calibrated and less dramatic.

## When To Use
Use this when headlines, anecdotes, one-off failures, or emotionally charged evidence are pushing you toward overreaction.

## Failure Mode
Using fake priors, ignoring conditional context, or pretending that a precise number means the judgment is sound.
