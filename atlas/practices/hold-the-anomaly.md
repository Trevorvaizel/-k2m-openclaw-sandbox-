# Hold the Anomaly

> When the frame fails, the discipline is not to reframe immediately. It is to hold the failure long enough for the next frame to form from the territory.

## Definition
Hold the Anomaly is a structured decommissioning practice drawn from Schön's reflection-in-action. When a map fails — when the territory produces a result that surprises the frame — the practitioner writes down exactly what surprised them and why, in the most concrete terms available, then resists importing the nearest alternative frame. The new frame must emerge from the anomaly rather than being applied to it.

## The Practice
1. When a map or framework produces a surprising result, stop before reframing.
2. Write down exactly what surprised you and why, in the most concrete terms available. No abstraction yet.
3. Identify which specific expectation the territory violated. Name the expectation, not just the surprise.
4. Do not import the nearest available alternative frame. Sit with the anomaly without resolving it.
5. Let the reframe emerge from the anomaly. A frame that comes from the anomaly carries territory contact; a frame imported from elsewhere may only paper over it.

## Why This Matters
The pull to reframe immediately is strong — the mind prefers a new map over no map. But premature reframing imports the nearest available alternative, which may share the same blind spot as the failed frame. The waiting is the skill. This is Decommissioning (Force XI) as a practice: the discipline is in not-knowing long enough for the new map to have room to form with genuine territory contact.

## Force Connections
- Operationalizes Decommissioning Force (Force XI): the transitional moment between frames
- Activates Antaeus Reality Contact: the anomaly is the ground — holding it is how the next map stays earthed
- Guards against Map-Duplicity Force (Force XIII): the anomaly often lives at the gap between espoused and operative map

## Cadence
Any time a map, framework, or diagnosis produces a clearly unexpected result. Especially before declaring a force settled or a manifesto section complete.

## Source
Schön, Donald. *The Reflective Practitioner: How Professionals Think in Action* (1983). Basic Books. — reflection-in-action and frame reframing as disciplined not-knowing. The practitioner "becomes a researcher in the practice context" rather than applying established technique.
