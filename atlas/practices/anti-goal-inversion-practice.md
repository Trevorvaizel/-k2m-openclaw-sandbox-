# Anti-Goal Inversion Practice

> Define the failure path first, then remove the brake before adding more force.

## Purpose
Use anti-goal inversion when motivation, inspiration, or optimization is not fixing the result. The practice converts a desired outcome into a concrete list of ways you are currently guaranteeing failure.

## Process
- State the goal in plain terms.
- Invert it: what would reliably produce the opposite?
- List the frictions, routines, blind spots, and single-point failures that fit that inverted outcome.
- Identify the biggest parking brake or zero.
- Remove that brake before adding more motivation, complexity, or horsepower.

## Result
- The real blocker becomes visible.
- Energy shifts from vague self-encouragement to direct obstacle removal.
- Systems improve by preventing failure instead of glamorizing success.

## When To Use
Use this when a habit keeps failing, a team keeps over-optimizing the wrong thing, or a system has obvious upside but still collapses in practice.

## Failure Mode
Stopping at a dramatic anti-goal without changing the actual structure. If the friction remains, the inversion stayed rhetorical.
