# Lattice

> A lattice is the cross-disciplinary structure that makes isolated knowledge reusable.

## Definition
The lattice is the structure of tested connections between models. Its density matters because new knowledge becomes faster to learn and easier to use when it attaches to strong existing nodes, but the value comes from connection quality rather than raw link count.

## Pattern
- Isolated facts stay inert.
- A tested connection turns a fact into a reusable model.
- Cross-domain links increase transfer speed and reduce single-model blindness.
- Decorative links create [[danger-lattice-overload]].

## Source-Grounded Example
Transcript 10 frames the latticework as the reason a variety of models beats a single favorite theory. It uses the image of different models handling different trains on the same track, then grounds the whole idea in the Antaeus warning: even a rich lattice decays when it loses contact with reality.

## Diagnostic / Use
Ask: Is this idea connected to strong concepts, or am I just making the graph denser?

## Trap or Limitation
Confusing many links with meaningful understanding. A dense lattice of weak concepts is worse than a sparse map.
