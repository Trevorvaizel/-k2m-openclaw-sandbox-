# Trust Gradient

> *Trust is not binary. It is a gradient â€” five layers that must be built in sequence. No shortcut exists.*

## The Five Layers

### Layer 1: Awareness
The audience recognizes their problem has a name they didn't know existed.
**What breaks it:** Leading with the product. The audience doesn't yet know they have a thinking problem.

### Layer 2: Credibility
The audience believes you understand their world more accurately than they understood it themselves.
**What breaks it:** Empathy language without specificity. Generic "we understand" without evidence.

### Layer 3: Competence
The audience believes you can actually deliver the resolution.
**What breaks it:** Showing only finished products. The audience needs to see the process, not just the result.

### Layer 4: Integrity
The audience believes you will not exploit their vulnerability.
**What breaks it:** "Trust the process." This asks the audience to surrender agency. Integrity is showing restraint.

### Layer 5: Identity
The audience believes that choosing you says something true about who they are or are becoming.
**What breaks it:** Asking for identity alignment before layers 1â€“4 are established.

## The Universal Rule
> *No amount of tactical optimization at the wrong layer moves the needle.*
