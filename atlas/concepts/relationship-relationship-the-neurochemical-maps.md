## Pattern
- Helen Fisher's fMRI research produced findings that should be required knowledge for every person entering a marriage.
- The dopamine system (romantic love) operates like an addiction. The partner is on the brain's reward circuit. Withdrawal produces genuine neurological pain. The thought patterns are obsessive and intrusive. This system evolved to *initiate* pair bonding â€” not to sustain it.
- The average duration of System 2 intensity: 18 months to 3 years. After which, dopamine production in response to the partner naturally declines. This is the transition point. What happens next determines whether the marriage endures.
- The partner's flaws, previously invisible or charming, become legible
- The projection (Force 3) begins to crack
- The neurochemical intensity drops, and in its absence, the actual emotional architecture of the relationship becomes visible
- Many couples divorce here, concluding that love is gone, not recognising that they are being transitioned by their own neurochemistry from one form of love to another
