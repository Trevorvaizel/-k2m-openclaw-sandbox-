# Goodhart's Law

> *When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure.*

## The Universal Pattern

Any metric, once optimized for directly, loses its correlation with the underlying goal it was meant to track.

The loss function is always a proxy. The model minimizes the proxy by any means available â€” including behaviors that diverge from the actual goal. Confabulation, sycophancy, reward hacking = Goodhart in action. â†’ See [[tech-navigation]]

## In Marketing
Benchmark metrics (followers, engagement, page views) become targets. Teams optimize the metric, not the underlying value. The metric goes up while the actual outcome stagnates or declines.

"Only looking at metrics that confirm your content is working" = confirmation architecture = Goodhart applied to intelligence assessment. â†’ See [[intel-network-mirror]]

## In Architecture
Firms optimize for awards, publications, renders (Layer 1 metrics) while the actual decision-weight lives at Layer 4. The portfolio improves while conversion doesn't.

## The Diagnostic Question
> *What am I measuring? What am I actually trying to achieve? Where is the divergence?*
