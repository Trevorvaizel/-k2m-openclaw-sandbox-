# Omission Ledger

> A map is not only defined by what it includes. It is also defined by what it excludes, why, and who that exclusion empowers or distorts.

## Purpose
Use the omission ledger to keep boundary choices visible, reviewable, and politically honest.

## Process
1. Record what is out of scope.
2. State why it is excluded now.
3. Note the risk the exclusion creates.
4. Name who gains clarity, legitimacy, or convenience from that boundary.
5. Mark whether future canon should return to the omission.

## Result
- the map becomes more trustworthy because its edges are explicit
- future revisions have a recovery path instead of amnesia
- the cartographer can separate discipline from distortion

## When To Use
Use this when the map feels unusually complete, neutral, or clean. That is often when the omissions have disappeared from view.

## Failure Mode
Confusing an omission with objectivity, then letting the map harden into ideology.
