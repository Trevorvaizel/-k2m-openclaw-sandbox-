# Progress Interview

> Interview for the job, the struggle, the desired progress, the abandoned alternatives, and the emotional cost of staying stuck.

## Purpose
Use the progress interview to move from feature demand to the real progress the user is trying to hire.

## Process
Interview for:
1. the job
2. the struggle
3. the desired progress
4. the abandoned alternatives
5. the emotional cost of staying where the user is

## Result
- requests become legible as progress-seeking behavior
- feature ideas get tied to underlying jobs
- user truth becomes more causally useful

## When To Use
Use this when users keep asking for things that feel concrete but do not explain why they matter.

## Failure Mode
Collecting vivid stories without converting them into product judgment.
