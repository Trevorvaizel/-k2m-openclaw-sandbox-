## Pattern
- Architecture firms operate on a set of assumptions about how clients choose. Every one of these assumptions is structurally incomplete.
- Assumption 1: "Our work speaks for itself"
- The Belief System Manifesto identifies this as a belief gap diagnosis failure. The firm believes the portfolio bridges the belief gap. It does not.
- A portfolio shows Layer 1 â€” the visible output. The client looking at a portfolio cannot see:
- Whether the architect listened to the previous client or overrode them
- Whether the process was structured or chaotic
- Whether their own taste will be respected or corrected
- Whether they will feel stupid for not knowing architectural vocabulary
- The portfolio answers "Can they design?" It does not answer "Will they understand me?" And "Will they understand me?" is the question that governs the decision.
