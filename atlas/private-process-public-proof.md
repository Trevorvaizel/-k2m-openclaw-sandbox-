# Private Process Public Proof

## Definition

Private process public proof is the design principle that students should do their messy thinking in protected spaces and reveal only the proof objects or finished expressions that can safely support community learning.

## Pattern

In K2M:

- DM and guided tool interactions hold the private process
- `#thinking-lab` is a bounded tool surface rather than a public performance stage
- `#thinking-showcase` makes finished proof visible
- peer visibility is designed around variety, pattern recognition, and celebration rather than ranking
- progressive disclosure reveals more of the social/product surface as readiness grows

This protects psychological safety without losing the social power of visible transformation.

## Diagnostic Use

Ask:

- Is this student being asked to perform before they have a safe place to think?
- What should stay private because it is still fragile?
- What should become public because community proof strengthens belief?

## Trap

The trap is either extreme: keeping everything private and losing social proof, or forcing premature publicness and turning the cohort into performance theater.
