# Belief Audit Tool

> A structured way to surface hidden beliefs, trace their origins, test them against reality, and decide whether to keep, revise, or replace them.

## Definition
The belief-audit tool is a practical synthesis built from [[agm-belief-revision]], [[predictive-processing]], and context-specific artifact reading. It treats beliefs as operating models that shape behavior and perception, then gives the user a way to inspect those models directly.

## Pattern
- A person keeps repeating a result, identity story, or decision pattern they do not fully understand.
- The underlying belief is surfaced through language, artifacts, and repeated framing.
- The belief is traced to origin, then tested against real evidence.
- The user chooses whether to keep it, revise it, or replace it with a better model.

## Source-Grounded Example
In the Kenyan-student context, the audit can use course choices, exam artifacts, and language patterns to surface beliefs such as "my grade defines my ceiling" or "people like me do not belong in this path." The operational move is not abstract reflection but structured revision grounded in real artifacts and alternative evidence.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "What belief is governing this pattern, where did it come from, and what evidence would make me revise it?"

Use this tool for coaching, transition moments, educational redirection, and any setting where the visible behavior is downstream of a hidden self-model.

## Trap
Turning belief audit into performance. If the user learns to say the updated belief without changing action, the structure did not actually revise.
