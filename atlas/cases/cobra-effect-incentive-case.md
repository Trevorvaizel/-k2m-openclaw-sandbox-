# Cobra Effect Incentive Case

> A policy can produce the opposite of its stated goal when it rewards the wrong behavior.

## Definition
The cobra-effect case shows how a clean first-order objective can generate a dirty second-order incentive landscape.

## Pattern
- A visible problem creates pressure for action.
- A metric or bounty is introduced to reduce it.
- Actors optimize the reward instead of solving the problem.
- The system becomes worse than before the intervention.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript recounts the dead-cobra bounty that led people to breed cobras for cash. When the bounty ended, the bred snakes were released, worsening the original problem.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "What behavior does this intervention make profitable?"

Use this case for policy, compensation design, product incentives, and any target that can be gamed.

## Trap
Treating the case as a warning against incentives altogether. The real warning is to design incentives with adaptive behavior in mind.
