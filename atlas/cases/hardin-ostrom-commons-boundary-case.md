# Hardin-Ostrom Commons Boundary Case

> A model becomes dangerous when its boundary conditions disappear from view.

## Definition
The Hardin-Ostrom commons boundary case shows why a persuasive model should not be treated as a universal law. Hardin's tragedy of the commons map depends on specific assumptions; Ostrom's fieldwork found real communities that managed shared resources through norms, trust, and local rules.

## Pattern
- A model isolates one incentive structure.
- The model becomes memorable and persuasive.
- Users forget the conditions that make the model valid.
- Field evidence reveals territory the model excluded.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript presents Hardin's open-pasture story as a logical model, then contrasts it with Eleanor Ostrom's studies of real commons such as Alpine meadows, forests, and irrigation systems where communication and social norms changed the outcome.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "What conditions must be true for this model to work, and do those conditions exist here?"

Use this case when applying economic, strategic, or organizational models across contexts.

## Trap
Replacing one universal claim with another. The point is not that communities always self-govern well; the point is that boundary conditions decide whether the map fits.
