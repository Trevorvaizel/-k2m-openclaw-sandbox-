# Hot Stove Feedback Distance Case

> The farther decision-makers are from consequences, the easier it is to preserve a bad map.

## Definition
The hot-stove feedback case explains why direct consequence updates behavior faster than delayed, filtered feedback. Distance gives ego time to protect the existing map.

## Pattern
- A person gets immediate painful feedback and updates quickly.
- A distant decision-maker gets delayed, filtered feedback.
- The delay creates room for narrative defense.
- Bad strategy survives because consequence never reaches the map clearly.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript contrasts touching a hot stove with an executive whose strategy fails 18 months later in a distant division. The executive has more room to blame the market, execution, or other filters.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "How far is this decision from its feedback loop?"

Use this case when evaluating leadership decisions, strategy reviews, or source reliability.

## Trap
Assuming seniority creates clearer maps. The transcript warns that higher positions often increase distance from hot-stove feedback.
