# Everest Local Guide Case

> Outside your circle, survival depends less on confidence than on finding a real local guide.

## Definition
The Everest local-guide case shows that surface preparation cannot replace lived terrain knowledge. Reading, gear, and vocabulary do not reveal the hidden variables that a lifer notices automatically.

## Pattern
- A person studies a difficult environment from the outside.
- The study creates a convincing sense of readiness.
- Hidden variables remain invisible because they are learned only through lived contact.
- The outsider survives only by borrowing competence from a true local guide.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript contrasts a reader of Everest books with Sherpa knowledge such as weather reading, route timing, and environmental instability. The point is not that outsiders can never climb; it is that successful passage depends on local guidance.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "Who is the lifer in this environment, and have I actually deferred to the parts they know that I cannot see?"

Use this case when entering a new market, role, system, or technical terrain with high hidden downside.

## Trap
Confusing borrowed jargon with borrowed competence. Knowing the words does not mean you inherited the guide's judgment.
