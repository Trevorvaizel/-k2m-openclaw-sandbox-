# Nansen Drift Design Case

> Sometimes the winning design works by cooperating with the force that seems to block progress.

## Definition
The Nansen drift-design case shows inversion as force alignment: stop designing against the environment when the environment can be used as the vehicle.

## Pattern
- A system keeps fighting a dominant external force.
- Direct resistance repeatedly fails.
- The problem is inverted to ask how the force can be used instead.
- The design changes from opposition to alignment.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript recounts Nansen designing a ship that would rise with Arctic ice and drift rather than be crushed by it. The force that threatened the mission became the mechanism that carried it.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "Which force am I wasting energy resisting that could become the vehicle instead?"

Use this case for product design, political strategy, market entry, and habit design where a constraint might be turned into the route.

## Trap
Romanticizing surrender. The case is about disciplined alignment with a real force, not passivity.
