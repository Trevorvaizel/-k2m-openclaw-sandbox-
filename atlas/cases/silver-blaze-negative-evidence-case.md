# Silver Blaze Negative Evidence Case

> Sometimes the missing event carries more signal than the visible one.

## Definition
The Silver Blaze case makes inversion concrete by treating the absence of an expected signal as evidence.

## Pattern
- Attention goes to the visible anomaly.
- A missing expected reaction is ignored.
- The absence narrows the field more than the obvious noise.
- The cause becomes legible through what did not happen.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript uses Sherlock Holmes's dog-that-did-not-bark case to show how inversion notices negative evidence rather than only cataloging positive clues.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "What should have happened here if the default story were true?"

Use this case in debugging, investigation, hiring, and intelligence work where normal responses can falsify a favored explanation.

## Trap
Projecting significance into every silence. The missing event matters only if it was genuinely expected under the baseline story.
