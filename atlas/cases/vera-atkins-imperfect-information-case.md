# Vera Atkins Imperfect Information Case

> Good probabilistic judgment often means acting without certainty while refusing to act without calibration.

## Definition
The Vera Atkins case shows how probability thinking works under severe information scarcity: no clean proof, no full verification, only priors, signals, and costly decisions.

## Pattern
- The decision-maker faces high stakes with incomplete evidence.
- Base requirements narrow the field before intuition enters.
- Small cues update belief without pretending to certainty.
- Action still carries real loss risk even after good process.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript describes Vera Atkins assessing wartime agents with almost no verified information. Fluency, demeanor, and other signals were used to update judgment under conditions where certainty was impossible and mistakes were fatal.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "What are the minimum priors, and which signals actually deserve to move them?"

Use this case for hiring, intelligence work, partner selection, and operational vetting under uncertainty.

## Trap
Retrofitting certainty after the fact. Good probabilistic process can still lose.
