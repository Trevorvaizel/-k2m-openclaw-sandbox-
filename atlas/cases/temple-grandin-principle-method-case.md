# Temple Grandin Principle-Method Case

> Methods change; the principle they serve is what should stay stable.

## Definition
The Temple Grandin principle-method case shows how easy it is to confuse a successful tactic with the principle that made it work. A curved chute is not the principle; low-stress animal handling is.

## Pattern
- A tactic solves a real problem and becomes popular.
- People copy the tactic as if it were the rule itself.
- New evidence questions the tactic.
- The deeper principle survives and allows better methods to emerge.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript uses Temple Grandin's curved cattle chute to show that the point was never curvature for its own sake. The point was reducing animal stress by controlling sight lines and movement flow.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "Am I defending the principle, or just defending the tactic that once served it?"

Use this case when a team is emotionally attached to a workflow, format, or design pattern that may no longer fit the job.

## Trap
Throwing away the principle along with the tactic. The case argues for method flexibility, not principle collapse.
