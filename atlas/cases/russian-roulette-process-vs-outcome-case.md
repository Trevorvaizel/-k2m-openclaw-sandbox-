# Russian Roulette Process vs Outcome Case

> A lucky result does not turn a bad process into a good one.

## Definition
The Russian-roulette case is a compact reminder that process quality and outcome quality diverge under uncertainty.

## Pattern
- A person takes a reckless action with asymmetric downside.
- The action happens to succeed this time.
- The success invites the false conclusion that the process was smart.
- The next repetition reintroduces the original risk unchanged.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript uses Russian roulette to show why "it worked" is not enough. The bullet not firing only proves luck arrived this time; it says nothing good about the method.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "If I repeated this process many times, would I still respect it?"

Use this case for investing, hiring, operations, and any decision where variance can flatter bad judgment.

## Trap
Overcorrecting into total risk aversion. The lesson is to evaluate process under repeat exposure, not to avoid all uncertainty.
