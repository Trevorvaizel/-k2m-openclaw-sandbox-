# Karima Abud Cartographer Bias Case

> A map often records the cartographer's prior story before it records the territory.

## Definition
The Karima Abud cartographer-bias case shows how observer incentives and expectations shape what a map captures. The same place can be represented as ancient fantasy or as a living modern society depending on who is holding the frame.

## Pattern
- An outsider arrives with a preferred story.
- The map is composed to confirm that story.
- Contradictory signs are cropped, staged away, or ignored.
- A different cartographer reveals territory the first map made invisible.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript contrasts European photographers staging Palestine as a biblical scene with Karima Abud's local photographs of ordinary modern life, including families, middle-class scenes, and women driving cars.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "Whose story does this map make easy to believe, and what would a local counter-map reveal?"

Use this case when reviewing market research, political framing, customer personas, or any evidence package produced by an interested observer.

## Trap
Assuming local perspective is automatically pure. The useful move is not to crown one cartographer; it is to audit viewpoint, incentive, omission, and counter-map.
