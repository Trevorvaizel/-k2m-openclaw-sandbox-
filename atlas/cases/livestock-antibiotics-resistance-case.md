# Livestock Antibiotics Resistance Case

> A small recurring first-order gain can hide a delayed system-wide burn.

## Definition
The livestock-antibiotics case shows how second-order costs can accumulate far from the site of the original decision.

## Pattern
- A repeated intervention creates an immediate local benefit.
- The system adapts slowly in the background.
- The delayed adaptation shifts the cost onto other people or the future.
- The apparent optimization becomes a public or long-horizon liability.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript describes low-dose livestock antibiotics that improve short-term growth while quietly selecting for antibiotic-resistant bacteria whose downstream costs are paid much later and elsewhere.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "Who pays for this gain later, and how far away from the decision does the burn happen?"

Use this case when immediate savings or growth look unusually cheap.

## Trap
Missing the time delay and concluding the system is healthy because no visible failure has arrived yet.
