# Russell Turkey Trend-Law Case

> A long successful trend is not the same thing as a law of reality.

## Definition
The Russell turkey case shows the danger of mistaking repeated experience for underlying truth. The turkey has overwhelming historical evidence that the farmer brings food, right up until the day the hidden purpose of feeding becomes visible.

## Pattern
- A repeated pattern creates confidence.
- Confidence hardens into a law-like belief.
- The observer ignores the mechanism that generated the pattern.
- A regime change arrives and the old model fails catastrophically.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript retells Bertrand Russell's turkey problem: day after day the turkey is fed, builds statistical confidence, and then discovers on the eve of Thanksgiving that the pattern was never the principle.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "What trend am I treating as a law, and what hidden mechanism could reverse it abruptly?"

Use this case when decisions rely heavily on backtests, recent growth, historical stability, or "this has always worked."

## Trap
Becoming paralyzed by uncertainty. The case is not an argument against patterns; it is an argument for asking what is generating them.
