# Atul Gawande Coaching Blind Spot Case

> Even inside a real circle of competence, blind spots accumulate without external observation.

## Definition
The Atul Gawande coaching case shows that competence can plateau when a practitioner can no longer see their own drift. External feedback is not only for beginners; it is how experts keep a deep circle from going stale.

## Pattern
- A person becomes highly competent in a domain.
- Repetition creates fluency but also normalizes invisible technique errors.
- A coach or observer watches the practitioner instead of the task.
- Small corrections reopen learning and improve results.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript describes Gawande hiring a coach to observe his surgery practice. The coach was able to see blind spots in setup and technique that Gawande himself no longer noticed.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "Where has fluency made me uninspectable, and who is watching me instead of only watching the output?"

Use this case when performance has plateaued in a domain where you are already experienced.

## Trap
Assuming expertise eliminates the need for critique. The case argues the opposite: the deeper the circle, the more valuable calibrated outside observation becomes.
