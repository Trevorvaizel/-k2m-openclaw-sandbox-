# Barry Marshall Bacterial Surprise Case

> A field can defend a false first principle for decades if everyone mistakes theory for territory.

## Definition
The Barry Marshall bacterial-surprise case shows how a foundational assumption can block reality contact. The medical map said the stomach had to be sterile; the territory contained bacteria anyway.

## Pattern
- A strong explanatory model hardens into dogma.
- Contradictory evidence appears but is dismissed as impossible or contaminated.
- An outsider questions the underlying assumption instead of patching the theory.
- Direct contact with reality falsifies the old map and reorganizes the field.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript follows Robin Warren and Barry Marshall finding H. pylori in ulcer patients, facing ridicule because bacteria "could not" survive stomach acid, and Marshall ultimately drinking the bacterial broth to prove the map was wrong.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "What impossibility claim in this field is really just a long-protected assumption?"

Use this case when expert consensus keeps rejecting inconvenient evidence.

## Trap
Romanticizing contrarianism. The lesson is not "outsiders are always right"; it is "claims about reality must stay vulnerable to evidence."
