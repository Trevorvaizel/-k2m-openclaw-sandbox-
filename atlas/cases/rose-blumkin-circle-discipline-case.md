# Rose Blumkin Circle Discipline Case

> Competence is defined as much by what you refuse as by what you pursue.

## Definition
The Rose Blumkin case shows perimeter discipline. Deep competence is not just knowing where to act quickly; it is refusing attractive opportunities that sit outside lived understanding.

## Pattern
- A person builds visceral knowledge in one specific domain.
- Opportunity appears in an adjacent field that looks profitable from the outside.
- The person refuses the move because the required judgment is not yet real.
- Long-run success comes from disciplined omission, not universal ambition.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript uses Nebraska Furniture Mart and Buffett's deal with Rose Blumkin to show that she understood furniture, pricing, and inventory at an instinctive level but would not buy stocks she did not understand, even when others saw easy upside.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "What am I touching here because it looks attractive, not because I have visceral judgment?"

Use this case when evaluating expansion, adjacent offers, new investments, or prestige opportunities.

## Trap
Turning the case into an argument for permanent narrowness. The lesson is disciplined perimeter definition, not lifelong refusal to learn.
