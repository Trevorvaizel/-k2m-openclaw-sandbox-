# Case Study 4: THE ATHLETE WHO SLOWED DOWN

> Force 5: Emergent Complexity

## Definition
Force 5: Emergent Complexity

## Pattern
- Timothy Gallwey (The Inner Game of Tennis) discovered: Trying harder makes you worse.
- Tennis players who thought about technique performed worse
- Players who focused on feel (not mechanics) performed better
- The whole (athletic performance) CANNOT be found in the parts (individual techniques)
- Emergence: Techniques combine to create properties none contain
- Attention economics: Focus on outcome, not mechanics = better performance
- Layer 4: Invisible "feel" matters more than visible mechanics
- You cannot master complexity by assembling parts. Focus on the emergent whole, not the individual techniques.
