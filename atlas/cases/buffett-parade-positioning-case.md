# Buffett Parade Positioning Case

> Relative position can stay unchanged even when everyone expends more effort.

## Definition
The Buffett parade case shows that some gains vanish once every participant reacts the same way.

## Pattern
- A crowded system rewards relative position rather than absolute effort.
- One actor improves their stance.
- Others copy the move.
- Total effort rises while the relative advantage disappears.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript uses Buffett's parade image: when one person stands to see better, others stand too, and soon nobody sees the parade any better than before.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "If everyone copied this move, would the advantage still exist?"

Use this case for competitive markets, signaling races, credential inflation, and any strategy that may trigger imitation.

## Trap
Confusing visible activity with structural advantage. The crowd can expend more energy without changing the view.
