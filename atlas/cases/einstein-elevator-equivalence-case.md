# Einstein Elevator Equivalence Case

> A carefully designed simulation can reveal a hidden law before direct measurement is possible.

## Definition
The Einstein elevator case shows how a thought experiment can expose an equivalence between two situations that look different on the surface.

## Pattern
- A mechanism is hard to observe directly.
- Two constrained scenarios are imagined side by side.
- The shared pattern becomes visible through the comparison.
- The simulation produces insight that later reality can test.

## Source-Grounded Example
The transcript asks whether a person in deep space accelerating upward could tell the difference from a person standing in gravity. The thought experiment reveals the equivalence that helped Einstein reason toward general relativity.

## Diagnostic/Use
Ask: "What paired scenarios would force the hidden mechanism to reveal itself?"

Use this case when a direct experiment is too costly, impossible, or premature.

## Trap
Treating the simulation as final proof. Its value is disciplined insight, not immunity from later testing.
