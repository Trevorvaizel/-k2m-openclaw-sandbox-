# Predictive Processing

> *The brain doesn't passively receive reality. It actively constructs experience from predictions, then updates when the predictions fail.*

## Definition

Predictive Processing (Friston, Clark) proposes that the brain is fundamentally a prediction engine. It has four components:

1. **Generative models** — internal models trained on experience, constantly refined
2. **Predictions** — the generative models predict what you're experiencing right now. Your *experience itself* is constructed from these predictions
3. **Prediction errors** — the gap between prediction and incoming sensory signal. When large enough, this error propagates upward
4. **Precision weightings** — how much attention/credibility to give each prediction error. This determines which errors trigger updates

The staggering implication: we have **4x more neural connections running FROM brain TO senses** than the other direction. The brain tells the eyes what to see more than the eyes tell the brain what's there.

## Invisible Mechanism

Beliefs are generative models. They literally construct your experience. The Remifentanil experiment proves this: a painkiller 200x stronger than morphine *completely stops working* if the patient believes they received a pain-increaser instead. Belief overrides chemistry at the neural level.

Similarly, smokers' reward circuitry responds to *belief about nicotine content*, not actual nicotine content. The belief IS the reward, not the substance.

## Cross-Domain Manifestations

- **Education**: A student who *believes* they're bad at math will predict failure, interpret ambiguous feedback as confirmation, and weight prediction errors that confirm their model higher than those that contradict it → [[beginners-mind|Beginner's Mind]] blocked
- **Marketing**: [[trust-gradient|Trust Gradient]] operates through precision weighting — low-trust audiences give low precision to your claims (they don't update)
- **Relationships**: [[moc-relationship-cartography|Relationship patterns]] persist because the generative model predicts partner behavior, filters perception to confirm, and downweights disconfirming evidence
- **Self-concept**: "I am my KCSE score" is a generative model that constructs every academic experience as evidence

## Diagnostic Question

*What am I predicting right now that's constructing my experience instead of letting me see what's actually here?*

## Trap

The [[danger-certainty-spiral|Certainty Spiral]] is a predictive processing trap: high confidence → high precision on predictions → low precision on errors → belief becomes immune to updating → certainty increases further. The brain becomes a closed loop.
